## 2. The Business in 5 Sentences (原则1 Test #1)

1. **Duolingo runs a freemium mobile language-learning app** with 56.5M DAUs / 137.8M MAUs (Q1'26), of which 12.5M (~9% of MAUs) pay $7-30/mo for Super or Max tiers.
2. **The growth engine is the free product's distribution flywheel** — gamification (streaks, leagues, hearts/energy) makes the app sticky enough that performance-marketing CAC stays low and word-of-mouth carries acquisition; ~85% global share of language-learning DAUs.
3. **Monetization is 86% subscription + 11% ads + 3% other** (Q1'26: $250.9M sub of $292M total); subscription revenue grew 31% YoY while bookings (the leading indicator) grew only 14% — a deliberate slowdown.
4. **The 2026 strategic pivot** explicitly trades near-term bookings growth (down to ~10.5% FY guide vs. 38% PY) for re-accelerating DAUs (~20% target) by removing friction from the free experience, lengthening free trials, and softening the energy/heart paywall — i.e., management chose to break the bookings line on purpose.
5. **The expansion thesis** is to extend the gamified model from languages into Math, Music, Chess (added in 9 months with AI assistance) and eventually become a multi-subject "learning Netflix," using AI both to manufacture content cheaply and as an in-app feature (Video Call with Lily, Roleplay) to deepen engagement and justify Max upsell.

**Pass?** ✅ Description-level yes. But "懂" per CLAUDE.md = predicting moat evolution, not describing current state. See §4.

## 4. 原则1 — 能力圈 (Capability Circle)

### The "懂" test, per CLAUDE.md upgrade
> **「懂」 = 能对这门生意的未来做出独立、可证伪的预判（壁垒会不会变、增长靠什么、什么会打破它）；只能描述现在、或复述别人对它未来的判断 = 圈外。**

### Three tests
| Test | Pass? | Honest answer |
|---|---|---|
| ① 5 sentences explain the business | ✅ | Done in §2. Can describe it cleanly. |
| ② Predict downside floor (independently, not from current cash level) | ⚠️ Partial | I can construct a floor (see §6 valuation) but the *load-bearing variable* is "does Duolingo's gamified habit moat survive LLM-native tutors in 2-3 years?" — and I cannot answer that without leaning on management's narrative or sell-side opinion. The floor is mechanically calculable; the floor *staying valid* depends on a moat call I can't make solo. |
| ③ 50% drop → 敢加 | ❌ | At $58 (50% from here, ~$2.7B mkt cap, ~5x EV/FCF on $350M+), the math screams cheap. But the reason it would be at $58 is almost certainly that DAU growth has snapped — which is exactly the kill-criterion. Buying into kill = anti-framework. |

### The honest existential question (Marks Model 3: AI tutoring is quantitative)
**Does gamification + behavioral lock-in beat a 1:1 LLM tutor with infinite patience and personalization, for the median language learner?**

The bear case in writing (TNW, Jan 2026):
> "ChatGPT can already conduct a fluent conversation in more than 50 languages, correct grammar in real time… Meta's Llama models, available open-source, allow any developer to build a language tutoring application with conversational AI at near-zero marginal cost. The technical moat that Duolingo built over a decade… is being commodified by AI models that can generate equivalent instruction on demand."

The bull rebuttal (management + bull-side analysts):
- ChatGPT launched Nov 2022 and DUOL still grew DAUs 21% YoY in Q1'26 — the substitution didn't happen
- Duolingo's moat is *not* the curriculum, it's the **streak/leaderboard habit loop** + free-tier distribution
- AI is a *cost-down ingredient* for DUOL (per-unit content costs are dropping fast — they shipped 20,500 units in Q1 vs. ~20K all of last year)

**My honest call:** Both can be true *for the next 18 months* but diverge sharply by year 3. Habit-driven retention can survive better tools (Spotify survived free YouTube music) — but Spotify had a clearer "we're not the music, we're the library + recommendation" wedge. Duolingo's wedge ("we're not the lessons, we're the streak") is real but thinner. The fact that paid subscriber growth is *exactly the same* as DAU growth (both 21%) means there is **zero leverage** between users and payers — every paid sub is just a fixed conversion off DAU. If DAU growth stalls for any reason (LLM, fatigue, missed product cycle), the whole business stalls. There is no second engine.

**Verdict on 原则1:** I am in the **(2) "可补习" but not yet (3) "晋级"** bracket. I can describe DUOL fluently but I cannot independently predict the LLM-substitution path with conviction. Per CLAUDE.md: 补习期表达方式仍是 ETF. **No single-stock entitlement.**

## 5. 原则2 — Four Dimensions + Moat

### Moat audit (the CLAUDE.md upgrade requires this *before* the four dimensions)
> **看生意先看壁垒：护城河是什么、护得住吗、未来两三年变强还是被侵蚀。**

| Source of moat | Strength | 2-yr trajectory |
|---|---|---|
| Gamification habit loop (streaks, leagues, daily push) | Real, hard to copy at scale | Stable to *slightly eroding* (TikTok-style attention competition is the actual zero-sum, not LLMs) |
| Free-tier distribution flywheel (low CAC) | Strong — 85% category share, dominant ASO | Stable, but Q1 call admitted "performance marketing only profitable in some geographies" — i.e., they're *near* the saturation point in core EN-speaking markets |
| Content library (250 courses) | Was a moat; AI is commodifying it | **Eroding** — they admit AI generates 20.5K units in one quarter |
| Brand / "the owl" | Genuine cultural asset (Duo memes, TikTok) | Stable, but brand can't monetize on its own |
| Switching cost | **Low** — no contract, low file/social cost | Same |
| Network effect | Mild (leaderboards) | Mild |

**Net moat call:** A **narrow, habit-based moat**, not a structural one. It is *not* getting stronger — the content-library leg is actively being commodified by general LLMs. The streak-loop leg is the actual remaining wall. That's defensible but it's *one* leg, and it requires the user to *care* about the gamification more than about actually learning the language. For most casual users (the vast majority of the 50M+ DAUs), that's true today. The risk is that for the *paying* cohort — adults who actually want fluency — a $20/mo Max tier vs. a $20/mo ChatGPT Plus subscription that does roleplay + corrections + cultural context becomes a real choice in 2027-2028.

## 7. Marks framework gap-check (Model 3: AI tutoring is quantitative)

Marks' framework asks: *which of {regulatory / brand / scale / network / habit / IP} survives the quantitative attack from AI?*

| DUOL moat leg | LLM exposure | Survives? |
|---|---|---|
| Curriculum/content | **High** — LLMs generate equivalent on demand | Eroding |
| Streak/habit loop | **Low** — gamification is a UX choice, not a content choice | Survives |
| Brand (Duo owl) | **Low** | Survives |
| Distribution / app-store ASO | Medium — depends on whether new AI apps can match free-tier quality | Mostly survives near-term |
| Switching cost | **Low** | Already low, doesn't change |

**Gap-check call:** Habit-loop moat *can* coexist with LLM tutors (just as Spotify coexists with YouTube and Audible). But the *paying* slice is where margins live, and paying users are more likely to A/B-compare value. **The risk is gradual ARPU erosion, not sudden death.** This is consistent with the 2-3 year deceleration shape, not a cliff.
