## 1. 原则1 — 能力圈 (Are we even allowed to play?)

This is the gate. The standard is **能对未来做出独立、可证伪的预判**, not "describe what they sell."

### Test 1: 5-sentence business explanation
1. CrowdStrike sells **Falcon**, a cloud-native cybersecurity SaaS that runs as a **single lightweight kernel-level sensor** on customer endpoints (laptops, servers, cloud workloads, identities, AI agents/VMs).
2. The sensor streams telemetry to CrowdStrike's cloud where AI models correlate trillions of events/week to detect & auto-prevent breaches; customers pay per-endpoint/identity/GB ingested, **subscription, ratable revenue**.
3. The land-and-expand engine is **modular add-ons** (~30+ Falcon modules) — cloud security, identity, SIEM, exposure mgmt, AIDR — bundled via **Falcon Flex**, an enterprise commitment that lets customers shift spend across modules.
4. Moat narrative: one sensor → one console → one platform; data network effect (more data → better detection → more wins → more data); switching cost = ripping a kernel agent off 100k+ endpoints.
5. The bear set: (a) **Microsoft Defender** is bundled with E5 and gaining share (Microsoft now ~25.8% mkt share vs CRWD ~18.1%); (b) July 2024 outage permanently damaged customer trust + still bleeding cost ($117.7M FY26, ongoing litigation); (c) valuation is priced for sustained 25%+ ARR growth + margin expansion + no AI commoditization — all three must hold.

**Pass test 1? Yes.** Description-level understanding is clean.

### Test 2: predict downside floor (bear case, falsifiable)

What can break this business in 24 months?
- **Trust scar reopens**: another major outage or a customer-facing security failure where Falcon was bypassed → mass non-renewal at large accounts.
- **Microsoft Defender bundling**: E5 already includes Defender for Endpoint; if Microsoft adds identity + SIEM equivalents at zero marginal price, CRWD churn at the lower/mid enterprise accelerates. CRWD gross retention is >97% today but renewal cohort effect lags 1–2 years.
- **AI commoditization of detection**: if open-source LLMs + sensor commodity makes "AI-native detection" a feature not a product (Marks' Model 3 applies — security is **interpretive judgment** more than commodity inference), CRWD's premium narrative deflates.
- **Multiple compression**: at 28x forward sales / 134x forward P/E / ~109x EV/FCF, if growth decel from 26% → 18% over 24 months, fair forward EV/sales likely compresses to 12-15x.

**Bear downside floor calculation (the only honest one):**
- FY28 revenue trough scenario: assume growth decels to 18% in FY28 → $5.94B × 1.18 = ~$7.0B revenue
- Bear forward EV/sales 12x → EV $84B → equity ~$87.6B → **~$344/share**
- Deeper bear (multiple compression to 10x + growth to 15%): EV ~$68B → **~$285/share**
- **Bear floor anchor: $280-$345** (from $658.68 today = **–48% to –57%**)

Note: bear floor is NOT zero — CRWD has $4.4B cash, $1.5B FCF, sticky enterprise contracts, 97% gross retention. It is a real business. The floor is **multiple compression**, not business destruction. But the multiple is the asset here.

**Pass test 2? Conditional pass.** I can name the floor mechanically but the conviction that customers won't churn in a recession + Microsoft consolidation pressure is **not independently verifiable from outside** — I'm leaning on CRWD's disclosure and analyst chorus. **This is borderline 圈外** — I can describe the moat narrative but I cannot independently predict whether Falcon's data moat survives Microsoft's bundling assault in 2027-2028.

### Test 3: 跌 50% 敢加?
At $329 (–50%), forward P/S would be ~14x. That's still expensive for a 18-22% grower with margin headwinds. Would I **independently** know that's a buy, or would I be hoping?
- **If the drop is multiple compression only** (growth still 22%+, ARR still accelerating) → yes, $329 is good value.
- **If the drop is fundamental** (Microsoft taking share, gross retention falling below 95%, AIDR pipeline not converting) → $329 could be the new normal and decline continues.

I can't distinguish these two scenarios independently of management commentary. **That's the圈外 signal.**

### 圈外/圈内 verdict
**Borderline 圈外, leaning 圈内 on description but 圈外 on prediction.** I understand the *current* business (description). I do NOT have independent thesis on whether Falcon's moat strengthens or erodes vs Microsoft Defender in FY28-FY30. The "AI security gateway" narrative is **borrowed from Jim Cramer / sell-side / Kurtz's earnings calls** — I cannot falsify it from raw evidence.

**Framework rule (CLAUDE.md):** *"只能描述现在、或复述别人对它未来的判断 = 圈外."* CRWD currently lands here for me.

**→ 原则1 verdict: 圈外 (or at best 补习期). Per framework: express via QQQ/SMH, no direct stock position until补习 completes.**
